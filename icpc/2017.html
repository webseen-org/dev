    Participation in Indian Regionals

    There is a change in the format of the ACM ICPC 2016 - 2017 Indian regionals. Starting this year, there will be 5 sites within India hosting the ICPC regionals. The 4 regional sites are - Amritapuri, Hindustan University, National Institute of Technical Teachers' Training and Research, Kolkata and IIT Kharagpur. On top of these, there will be an additional “India Final” to be held at IIITM Gwalior. Teams will be moving to the World Finals from all the 5 sites. Here’s everything you need to know about participation in Indian regionals.

    • Participants need to register on the Baylor Site to be eligible to take part in the contest. Click here to register.
    • There will only be one online round for all the 4 India regionals. There will not be any online round for “India-Final” held at IIITM Gwalior.
    • You must not register your team for more than two sites, as that will lead to your disqualification from all regionals. However, registration in “India Final” is exempt from this restriction.
    • A team in Asia can take part in regionals for 5 years (and not 5 regionals).
    • Based on performance of the online round, each regional site will decide on the number of teams to be invited for the on-site round.
    • Preference will be given to maximize the number of colleges participating in the on-site round.
    • Each regional site will have a few slots booked for advancing the top teams to the World Finals based on the performance of the teams in their on-site rounds. It is applicable for the “India Final” as well. More information on India Final can be found here: http://blog.sina.com.cn/s/blog_b946da100102wvei.html.
    • The teams for India Final will be selected directly from the onsite rounds of the 4 regional sites. Where “n” (the final number may vary from site to site) number of teams from each regional site will be allowed to take part in India Final thus giving them another shot at a slot to World Finals.
    • Participation in India Final will not be counted toward the restrictions of two Asia Regional Contests per year. This means that even if you have participated in two onsite regionals, you still can register and take part in India Final. However, if you do not register for the India Final, then your candidature will be invalid even if you are in the top “n” teams on a regional site and qualify for the India Final.
    • Non-India West Continent teams may compete for World Final slots in any India contest sites.  However, Non-India team competing for World Final slots in India-Final Contest site requires the approval from Hosting Committee of India-Final.

    How CodeChef is additionally contributing this year?

    Apart from the ambitious Go For Gold initiative, where we want an Indian team to win the ICPC world finals, for the 2016 - 2017 edition, we will continue our support towards the participants through:

    • Conducting two warm-up rounds for ACM ICPC on the Sunday, 18th September, 2016 and Sunday, 23rd October, 2016.
    • ACM-ICPC travel reimbursement for 75 Indian students from the September and October Cook-Off 2016's respectively.
      • Top 50 participants from September Cook-Off 2016 will be eligible for reimbursement, while new top 25 participants will be selected from October Cook-Off 2016.
      • In order to participate in the contest and stake your claims to go to any of the regionals, all you need is a CodeChef id. We guess you already have it. If you do not, then get one here.
    • On top of all, as a part our Go For Gold initiative, a team winning Gold medal at ACM ICPC World Finals will receive a cash prize of INR 1,178,890.
    How do I get my ACM-ICPC expenses reimbursed?
    • Register on any of the above sites
    • Participate in the Warm-Up Round for ACM ICPC 2016 - 2017.
    • Secure the rank within the eligibility criterion in any of the 2 Mega Warm Up Cook-Offs in the Indian Category
    • Send an email to goforgold@codechef.com with subjectline 'Reimbursement for ACM ICPC 2017' before the commencement of onsite contests.
    • That's it. Just 4 Simple Steps and you'll be on your way to having your ACM-ICPC expenses reimbursed by CodeChef.
    The Reimbursement of ACM-ICPC Expenses is subject to the following conditions:
    • Each winner shall be reimbursed an amount not greater than INR 1500 against the total expenses
    • The said amount shall be reimbursed against submission of valid proof (in original) E.g. 3rd AC Train & Bus Tickets, etc. Flight tickets will not be considered for travel reimbursement.
    • The expenses being claimed should have been borne only the winner of the Warm Up Cook-Offs alone.
    • Instances where there are expenses by people accompanying the winner shall not be eligible for reimbursement.
    • After the contest, send us an email to goforgold@codechef.com with subject "Reimbursement of ACM-ICPC expenses" and we'll get in touch.

    CodeChef - A Platform for Aspiring Programmers

At CodeChef we work hard to revive the geek in you by hosting a programming contest at the start of the month and two smaller programming challenges at the middle and end of the month. We also aim to have training sessions and discussions related to algorithms, binary search, technicalities like array size and the likes.
